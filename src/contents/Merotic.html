Prescribing Information

Indications: Respiratory tract infections (Community acquired penumonia & Nosocomial penumonia, Complicated intra-abdominal infections, Complicated Skin and soft tissue infections, Gynecological Infections, Surgical Infections, Bacterial meningitis, Complicated Urinary Tract Infections

Dosage & Administration:
Adult dose:The usual dose is 500 to 1 gm by intravenous infusion every 8 hours, depending on type and severity of infections, known or suspected suscesptibility of the pathogens and condition of the patients.
Pediatric dose:For children and infants over 3 months of age and weighing up to 50 kg the recommended dose of Meropenem is 10 to 40 mg/kg every 8 hours, depending on type and severity of infections, known or suspected suscesptibility of the pathogens and condition of the patients.

***Meropenem should be given by intravenous infusion over approximately 15 to 30 minutes or as an intravenous bolus injection (5 to 20 ml) over approximately 5 minutes.
Use in pregnancy & lactation:The safety of Meropenem in human pregnancy has not been evaluated. Animal studies have not shown any adverse effect on the developing foetus. Meropenem should not be used in pregnancy unless the potential benefit justifies the potential risk to the foetus. Meropenem is detectable at very low concentrations in animal breast milk. It should be used in lactating women unless the potential benefit justifies the potential risk to the baby.
