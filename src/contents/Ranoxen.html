Prescribing Information

Indications:
Ranoxen® is indicated for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis and juvenile rheumatoid arthritis. Ranoxen® is also indicated for the treatment of tendonitis, bursitis, acute gout, for the management of pain, primary dysmenorrheal, migraine treatment and prophylaxis
Dosage & Administration:
Rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis and ankylosing spondylitis:The recommended dose of Naproxen is 250 mg or 500 mg taken twice daily (morning and evening) or single daily dose of 500-1000mg given morning or evening, or 500 mg taken twice daily or a single daily dose of 1000 mg given in the evening. During long- time administration the dose of naproxen may be adjusted up or down depending on the clinical response of the patient
Acute gout: The recommended starting dose is of 750 mg, followed by 250 mg every 8 hours until the attack has subsided
Dysennorhoea: 500mg should be given initially, followed by 250 mg at 6 to 8 hours intervals for up to 5 days
Analgesia and acute muscular skeletal disorders: 500 mg should be given initially, followed by 250 mg at 6 to 8 hours intervals. Children over 5 years
Juvenile rheumatoid arthritis: 10 mg/ kg/ day given as 2 divided doses at 12 hours intervals
Use in pregnancy & Lactation : There are no well controlled studies in pregnant women. The drug should not be used during pregnancy unless clearly needed. Because of the possible adverse effects of prostaglandin inhibiting drugs on neonates, use in nursing mothers must be avoided
