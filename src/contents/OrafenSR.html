Prescribing Information

Indications:
Diclofenac Sodium is indicated i) For the relief of pain and inflammation in rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis. ii) Non-articular rheumatism, acute musculoskeletal disorders and back pain. iii) Painful syndromes of the vertebral column. iv) Acute gout. v) Painful post-traumatic and post-operative inflammation and swelling such as dental or orthopaedic surgery. vi) Painful inflammatory conditions in gynaecology and primary dysmenorrhea. vii) In fever and pain associated with inflammatory infections of the upper respiratory tract
Dosage & Administration:
One tablet daily, preferably at meal times or, as directed by the physician
Use in pregnancy & Lactation : Diclofenac Sodium should be used during pregnancy only if the benefits to the mother justify the potential risk to the fetus. Like other prostaglandin-inhibiting drugs, use of diclofenac during late pregnancy should be avoided. Following oral dose of 150 mg daily the active substance passes into the breast milk but in quantities so small that no undesirable effects are to be expected.
