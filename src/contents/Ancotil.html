Prescribing Information

Indications:
1. Emotional disturbance:Acute tension and anxiety states. Agitation, insomnia, anxiety and age related depressive reactions.
2. Functional disturbance in the cardiovascular respiratory system:pseudoangina pectoris, precordial anxiety, tachycardia, emotiogenic hypertension, dyspnea and hyperventilation.
3. In the gastrointestinal system: Irritable bowel syndrome, epigastria pain, spasm, blotting diarrhoea, etc.
4. In the genitourinary system:Irritable bladder and dysmenorrhea.
5. Psychosomatic disorders:psychogenic headache, psychogenic dermatitis, asthma, gastric and duodenal ulcer, ulcerative colitis. Emotional reactions to chronic organic diseases.
Dosage & Administration:
Use in adult:The optimum dosage and frequency of administration of Bromazepam should be based on the individual patient, the severity of symptoms and previous psychotropic drug history. The usual dosage in general practice is from 3 mg to 18 mg daily in divided doses. In exceptional circumstances, in hospitalized patients, up to the maximum daily dosage of 60 mg, in divided doses, may be given.
Use in Elderly:Elderly patients are more sensitive to the actions of Bromazepam. Doses should not exceed half of those normally recommended
Use in Children:Bromazepam is not recommended for paediatric use.
Use in Pregnancy: Use of benzodiazepines should be avoided during pregnancy, fetus may be affected. Bromazepam is secreted through human milk. So nursing mothers should not use this drug.
